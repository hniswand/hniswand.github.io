About Me

I am currently studying Computer Science at the University of Michigan in the College of the Literature, Science, and the Arts, where I have enjoyed taking a variety of courses that have expanded my knowledge and understanding of software engineering and programming. These courses have allowed me to learn various programming languages and fundamental programming topics.

My Experience

As an undergraduate instructional aide for the course EECS 280: Introductory Data Structures and Algorithms at the University of Michigan, I taught thirty undergraduate students by utilizing a wide range of instructional approaches. I helped structure course by drafting and grading exams, creating assignments, and hosting four hours of office hours per week for a course with over 1300 students. Additionally, through my coursework at the University of Michigan, I have learned about topics such as data structures and algorithms, machine learning, data analytics, and more. I am also active in campus clubs and communities that have expanded my skills beyond the classwork, such as Michigan Hackers and Girls in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science (GEECS).
